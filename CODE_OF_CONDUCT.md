# Tova Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Tova project is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are building a programming language. That work demands clarity of thought, technical rigor, and honest collaboration. This code of conduct exists to protect those values and the people who bring them.

## Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be direct and constructive.** Say what you mean. If code is wrong, say so clearly and explain why. Honest technical feedback — delivered with respect — is a gift.
- **Assume competence.** Treat other contributors as capable adults. Don't patronize. Don't over-explain unless asked.
- **Own your mistakes.** When you break something, say so. When you're wrong, acknowledge it. This earns trust faster than any amount of hedging.
- **Stay focused.** Keep discussions on-topic. Technical disagreements are welcome; personal attacks are not. Argue the idea, not the person.
- **Help others grow.** Review code carefully. Mentor when you can. Share knowledge without gatekeeping. A rising tide lifts all boats.
- **Respect people's time.** Write clear bug reports. Provide reproduction steps. Read the docs before asking. Do the work to make your contributions easy to review.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Trolling, deliberate derailing, or bad-faith arguments
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sexual language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or other project activities
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all Tova project spaces — GitHub repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, discussions, chat channels, meetups, conferences, and any other forum where you represent or participate in the Tova community.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces (e.g., using an official project email, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an event).

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the project maintainers at:

**conduct@tova-lang.org**

All reports will be reviewed promptly and confidentially. You will not face retaliation for making a good-faith report.

When reporting, please include:
- Your contact information
- Names (or usernames) of those involved
- Description of the behavior and when/where it occurred
- Any supporting evidence (screenshots, links, etc.)
- Whether you believe the situation is ongoing

### Process

1. **Acknowledgment.** Reports will be acknowledged within 48 hours.
2. **Investigation.** The enforcement team will review the report, gather context, and speak with relevant parties.
3. **Decision.** A resolution will be determined based on the severity and context of the violation.
4. **Communication.** The reporter will be informed of the outcome. Details shared will respect the privacy of all parties.

### Consequences

Enforcement actions are proportional to the severity and pattern of behavior:

**1. Warning**
A private written warning with an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**2. Temporary Suspension**
Temporary removal from project spaces (issues, PRs, discussions, chat) for a defined period. Violating the suspension terms may lead to a permanent ban.

**3. Permanent Ban**
Permanent removal from all project spaces. This is reserved for severe violations or repeated patterns of unacceptable behavior.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Conflict Resolution

Not every disagreement is a code of conduct violation. Technical disagreements are normal and healthy. Before filing a report, consider:

1. **Talk directly.** If you feel comfortable, address the issue with the person directly. Many conflicts arise from misunderstanding, not malice.
2. **Seek mediation.** Ask a maintainer to facilitate a conversation if direct communication isn't working.
3. **File a report.** If the behavior is clearly unacceptable or direct resolution has failed, file a formal report.
